## Core KPIs

### 1. Total Sales

**Formula:**

SUM(Sales)

**Business Meaning:**

Measures the total revenue generated by the business.

---

### 2. Total Profit

**Formula:**

SUM(Profit)

**Business Meaning:**

Shows the overall profit earned by the company.

---

### 3. Profit Margin

**Formula:**

(Profit ÷ Sales) × 100

**Business Meaning:**

Indicates how efficiently the company converts revenue into profit.

---

### 4. Average Order Value (AOV)

**Formula:**

Sales ÷ Number of Orders

**Business Meaning:**

Represents the average amount spent per order.

---

### 5. Total Orders

**Formula:**

COUNT(DISTINCT Order ID)

**Business Meaning:**

Measures overall business activity by counting unique orders.

---

## Deep-Dive Analysis: Customer Segmentation

### Business Question

Which customers contribute most to sales and profit?

### Segmentation Logic

Customers were segmented based on their total sales contribution.

* **High Value:** Customer sales above average customer sales.
* **Medium Value:** Customer sales around average customer sales.
* **Low Value:** Customer sales below average customer sales.

## Key Findings

* High-value customers contributed the majority of sales and profit.
* Medium-value customers presented opportunities for upselling and retention.
* Low-value customers formed a large portion of the customer base but generated comparatively lower revenue.
* Certain regions and categories outperformed others in profitability.
* Sales trends revealed seasonal variations in customer purchasing behavior.

---

## Recommendations

* Implement loyalty programs to retain high-value customers.
* Develop targeted marketing strategies for medium-value customers.
* Encourage repeat purchases among low-value customers through promotions.
* Focus investments on profitable categories and regions.
* Monitor KPI performance regularly to support strategic decisions.
